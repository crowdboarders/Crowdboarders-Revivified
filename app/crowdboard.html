Description:The Guards Museum of London is building a memorial garden monument to commemorate the centenial of WW1 and the battles on Flanders Field.
More Information
Get Involved in the Guards Museum Today!
Be part of history and have your name placed in the ‘Book of Honour’ being created by The Guards Museum close to Buckingham Palace. Show your appreciation to the hundreds of thousands of soldiers who perished in the First World War by donating to this Memorial that the millions of visitors to London can enjoy.

The Flanders Fields Memorial Garden 1914-2014’ is an initiative by The Guards Museum and supported by Flanders House in London. The Garden will be located at Wellington Barracks alongside The Guards Chapel which is adjacent to Buckingham Palace. The Memorial is a gift from the people of Belgium to the British people and will be a unique opportunity not only to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the start of The Great War but also to thank the British people for their great sacrifice in the liberation of Belgium.

The Memorial Garden has been designed by internationally acclaimed landscape architect Piet Blanckaert from Bruges, Belgium.
Your support for the Memorial will help strengthen the historical ties between Britain and Belgium as this important project seeks to bring home soil from the 70 battlefield cemeteries where soldiers of the seven Guards regiments fought and died for freedom 100 years ago. Your help will allow people of all nationalities and especially school children, to pay their respects to these war heroes in London. The project has already become the template for similar garden projects in France, India and Australia. The project is supported wholeheartedly by the Flemish government, the House of Lords, the City of Westminster, the British Army and English Heritage.

The Garden will be opened to the public during the Summer with an official opening on the 6th of November 2014 in a unique and moving ceremony which it is hoped will be attended by both countries’ Royal Families.
